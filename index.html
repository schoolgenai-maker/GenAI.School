About the School

The essential identity of the event: what, when, where, and how.

Welcome to the International School on Emerging Technologies of Generative AI, a fast-paced, bootcamp-style event designed to minimize slides and maximize hands-on coding and experimentation.

  • Date: May 4th, 5th, and 6th, 2026
  • Location: ENETCom, University of Sfax, Tunisia
  • Format: 5-hour sessions daily (9:00 AM – 2:00 PM).

Prerequisites

To get the most out of this hands-on bootcamp, participants should have a basic understanding of Python programming and general concepts of Machine Learning. Bring your laptop!

Program Schedule

The core value: exactly what you will learn and build, hour by hour.

Day 1 Foundations & Mastering LLMs

Objective: Understand what happens "under the hood" and learn how to operate a model professionally.

09:00 – 10:30

Theory: From Transformers to LLMs

Decoder-only architecture, tokens, context window, and decoding. Model lifecycle: pre-training and post-training.

10:30 – 11:30

Practice: Advanced Prompt Engineering

Hands-on techniques: few-shot prompting, Chain of Thought (CoT), and structured outputs (JSON).

11:30 – 12:00

Coffee Break

12:00 – 14:00

Theory & Practice: Fine-tuning vs. In-Context Learning

Demonstration of PEFT/LoRA. Workshop: Quick fine-tuning of a small model (e.g., Mistral/Llama 3) using Colab/Unsloth.

Day 2 RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

Objective: Reliably connect an LLM to private data.

09:00 – 10:30

Theory: The RAG Pipeline

Embeddings, vector databases, and cosine similarity. The hallucination problem and evaluation.

10:30 – 11:30

Practice: My First RAG Pipeline

PDF ingestion, chunking, storage, and simple retrieval using LangChain.

11:30 – 12:00

Coffee Break

12:00 – 14:00

Theory & Practice: Advanced RAG

Implementing optimization techniques: hybrid search, result re-ranking. Relevance evaluation (RAGAS).

Day 3 The Era of AI Agents

Objective: Move from a passive chatbot to an autonomous system capable of using tools.

09:00 – 10:30

Theory: Reasoning & Tools

The ReAct framework, Agent architecture, Agents vs. agentic systems, and common design patterns.

10:30 – 11:30

Practice: Tool Use & Function Calling

Teaching an LLM to call a specific Python function to solve tasks (e.g., math or weather).

11:30 – 12:00

Coffee Break

12:00 – 14:00

Final Application

Using frameworks like LangGraph or n8n. Workshop: Build a collaborative team of agents.

Target Audience

Ensuring this bootcamp is the perfect fit for your background and goals.

This program is carefully tailored for academic and research professionals looking to deepen their practical knowledge of Generative AI.

  • PhD Students: Looking to integrate advanced LLM tools and AI agents into their research methodologies.
  • Faculty & Teachers: Seeking to update their curriculum and explore modern AI capabilities.
